🚀 Google Analytics 4 Setup Guide

Your photography website is ready for GA4! Just follow these steps to activate tracking.

Step 1: Create GA4 Property

  1. Go to Google Analytics
  2. Sign in with your Google account
  3. Click "Create Account" or "Admin" (gear icon)
  4. Fill in:
    • Account name: "Oscar Espinoza Photography"
    • Property name: "Food Photography Website"
    • Industry: "Arts & Entertainment"
    • Business size: "Small"
  5. For Data Stream:
    • Platform: Web
    • Website URL: https://food.oscarespinoza.co.uk (your domain)
    • Stream name: "Food Photography Site"

Step 2: Get Your Measurement ID

After creating the property, you'll see a Measurement ID like:

G-XXXXXXXXXX

Copy this ID - you'll need it for the next step!

Step 3: Add Your Measurement ID

  1. Open file: includes/analytics.php
  2. Find line 4: $GA4_MEASUREMENT_ID = 'G-XXXXXXXXXX';
  3. Replace G-XXXXXXXXXX with your actual Measurement ID
  4. Save the file
⚠️ Important: The tracking only works on your live website, not on localhost:8000. This prevents development traffic from affecting your analytics.

Step 4: Set Up Conversion Goals

In your GA4 dashboard, go to Admin → Events → Create Event and set up these conversions:

📧 Lead Generation (Contact Form)

  • Event name: generate_lead
  • Description: Contact form submissions
  • Mark as conversion: ✅ Yes

💳 Purchase Intent (Stripe Deposits)

  • Event name: begin_checkout
  • Description: Stripe payment clicks
  • Mark as conversion: ✅ Yes

📞 Consultation Bookings

  • Event name: schedule_consultation
  • Description: Calendly booking clicks
  • Mark as conversion: ✅ Yes

🎯 What You'll Track

  • 📊 Visitor Analytics: Page views, session duration, bounce rate
  • 🎯 Lead Generation: Contact form submissions
  • 💰 Revenue Tracking: Stripe deposit payments
  • 📞 Consultation Requests: Calendly clicks
  • 🖼️ Portfolio Engagement: Image views by category
  • 📱 Device Data: Mobile vs desktop usage
  • 🌍 Geographic Data: Where your clients are located

Step 5: Test Your Tracking

  1. Upload your website to your live domain
  2. Visit your live website
  3. In GA4, go to Reports → Realtime
  4. You should see your visit tracked!
  5. Test conversions by:
    • Clicking "Pay Deposit" buttons
    • Submitting the contact form
    • Clicking "Book Consultation"

🔍 Development Mode

On localhost:8000, analytics events are logged to the browser console instead of GA4. Open DevTools → Console to see tracking events during development.

✅ Expected Results

Within 24-48 hours, you'll see:

  • Traffic sources: How people find your site (Google, social media, direct)
  • Popular pages: Which services/portfolio items get most views
  • Conversion rates: How many visitors become leads
  • Device preferences: Mobile vs desktop usage patterns
  • Geographic insights: Where your potential clients are located

📈 Business Impact: Use this data to optimize your marketing, pricing, and content strategy!
